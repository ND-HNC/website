\section{Summary}
This document establishes procedures for building access of the Notre Dame Human Neuroimaging Center (ND-HNC), located in the Lower Level of:\\

Veldman Family Psychology Clinic\\
501 North Hill St.\\
South Bend, IN 46617

\section{Scope}
These policies apply to all investigators, research assistants, and staff who use the ND-HNC.

\section{Notes}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item While the VFPC and ND-HNC are administratively independent, coordination is required as the entire building operates on the same alarm system. To avoid false alarms and their associated fees (from the South Bend Police Department), we use the VFPC logbook when entering/exiting to help ensure the alarm is set when appropriate.
\end{enumerate}


\section{Policies \& Procedures}

Procedures to access the building during and outside normal business hours, hosting visitors, and key/card access are detailed. For specific policies and procedures related to access of the MRI Zones, please refer to SOP 202.

% PIN numbers
\subsection{Set PIN}
\label{ssec:set-pin}
Two types of Personal Identification Numbers (PIN) are used at the VFPC and ND-HNC. First, a 4-digit PIN accompanies the Irish1Card for building access. Second, a 6-digit security PIN is used to de/activate the VFPC alarm system.

\begin{itemize}
	\item Set or access your Irish1Card PIN: \url{https://idcardpin.nd.edu}.
	\item Set or access your security PIN: Contact Building Management.
\end{itemize}

% Enter normal
\subsection{Entering Building: Business Hours}
\label{ssec:enter-norm}

Normal building business hours Monday through Thursday are 7am-7pm. Friday hours are 7am-4pm. The exterior door to the main entrance is unlocked during normal hours (Fig. \ref{fig:vfpc-main}).\\

The following are required for Business Hours access:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Irish1Card,
	\item 4-digit Irish1Card PIN,
\end{enumerate}

To enter the building during Business Hours:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Enter via main entrance}: access VFPC reception by using your Irish1Card and PIN at the anteroom inner door (Fig. \ref{fig:vfpc-main-inner}).
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item When present, the VFPC receptionist may let you in from their desk.
	\end{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Enter via staff entrance}: use your Irish1Card and PIN (Figs. \ref{fig:vfpc-staff-north}, \ref{fig:vfpc-staff-south}).
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Present your Irish1Card to the Electronic Lock (Fig. \ref{fig:vfpc-elec-lock}).
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item Red and green lights should flash
		\end{enumerate}
		\item Enter your Irish1Card PIN.
		\item If successful, the Electronic Lock will flash green and the door unlock.
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Sign in at the VFPC reception logbook (Figs. \ref{fig:vfpc-log1}, \ref{fig:vfpc-log2}).
	\item Proceed to your destination which may require the use of:
	\begin{enumerate}
        \item Your Irish1Card, for non-public areas,
        \item Physical key, for private offices and specific rooms.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Enter outside normal
\subsection{Entering Building: Extra Hours}
\label{ssec:enter-exnorm}

Extra building hours are any time not designated as Business Hours (SOP 301.\ref{ssec:enter-norm}). The exterior door of the main entrance will be locked during these times and access will only be possible via staff entrances. Additionally, the alarm system will need to be managed.\\

The following are required for Extra Hours access:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Irish1Card,
	\item 4-digit Irish1Card PIN,
	\item 6-digit security PIN.
\end{enumerate}


To enter the building during Extra Hours:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Enter via staff entrance (SOP 301.\ref{ssec:enter-norm}).
    \item Check the alarm keypad to determine whether the alarm is set (Fig. \ref{fig:vfpc-alarm}).
    \item To disable building alarm:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Enter your 6-digit code by touching the numbers on the keypad.
        \item Hit the `Enter' button to the top-right on the keypad.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Sign in at the VFPC reception logbook (Figs. \ref{fig:vfpc-log1}, \ref{fig:vfpc-log2}).
\end{enumerate}


% Host visitors
\subsection{Hosting Visitors}
\label{ssec:host-visit}

All Visitors arrive to, and are greeted at, the first floor reception area using the main entrance. The researcher/host can then help the Visitor navigate to ND-HNC.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Visitors can be let into the main entrance by:
	\begin{enumerate}
        \item Physically opening the door, or
        \item Using the wheelchair access buttons after the door is unlocked, or
        \item Using the phone/intercom system
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Visitors do not sign in at the front desk.
    \item Visitors must be escorted and monitored at all times.
    \item At the end of the visit, escort Visitors back to the reception area.
\end{enumerate}


% Exit building normal
\subsection{Exit Building: Normal Business Hours}
\label{ssec:exit-norm}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Sign out of logbook at first floor reception (Figs. \ref{fig:vfpc-log1}, \ref{fig:vfpc-log2}).
\end{enumerate}


% Exit building out normal
\subsection{Exit Building: Outside Normal Business Hours}
\label{ssec:exit-outnorm}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Sign out of logbook at first floor reception (Figs. \ref{fig:vfpc-log1}, \ref{fig:vfpc-log2}).
	\item Using the logbook, confirm whether anyone remains in the building.
	\item If last, activate building security (Fig. \ref{fig:vfpc-alarm}):
	\begin{enumerate}
        \item Verify the green checkmark appears on the keypad.
        \item Enter your 6-digit alarm code.
        \item Press `Enter'.
        \item Wait for `Alarmed', exit within 30 seconds.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Key access
\subsection{Key Access}
\label{ssec:keys}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Master keys: available only at lockbox (Fig \ref{fig:vfpc-lockbox}).
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Note: Master keys must \textbf{NOT} be shared.
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Room keys: contact ND-HNC staff.
\end{enumerate}
