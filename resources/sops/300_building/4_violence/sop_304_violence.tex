% Violence
\subsection{Active Violence is Occurring}
\label{ssec:active-violence}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Run!
	\begin{enumerate}
        \item Get away from the threat, leave your belongings behind.
        \item Save yourself, help others if possible.
        \item Call 911 when safe.
        \item Do \textbf{NOT} congregate.
    \end{enumerate}
	\item Hide!
	\begin{enumerate}
        \item If evacuation is not possible, find a place where the active shooter is less likely to find you.
        \item Within the building, find a room you can lock and barricade yourself inside using any equipment or furniture items.
        \item Silence your cell phone and remain quiet.
        \item Call 911 to alert authorities to the situation and possibly to the active shooter's location.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Fight!
	\begin{enumerate}
        \item If evacuation is not possible, find a place where the active shooter is less likely to find you. Within the building, find a room you can lock and barricade yourself inside using any equipment or furniture items.
        \item Silence your cell phone and remain quiet.
        \item Call 911 to alert authorities to the situation and possibly to the active shooter's location.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Police response
\subsection{Police Response to Active Violence Situations}
\label{ssec:police}

Be aware the number one priority for police is to stop the threat quickly. Therefore, police may:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Arrive in variable attire such as plain clothes or uniforms from other agencies.
	\item Be carrying weapons including rifles.
	\item Shout commands such as ``put your hands up''.
	\item Move past injured persons to locate and stop the active threat.
\end{enumerate}

Always comply with police instruction.


% No Guns
\subsection{No Guns Policy}
\label{ssec:no-guns}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item It is a University firearm policy that weapons are not allowed on Notre Dame property.
	\item If you see someone carrying a firearm, contact the Notre Dame Police Department (NDPD) at (574) 631-5555. NDPD will respond because it is a University firearm policy that is being violated, not a criminal response.
	\item Let the person know weapons are not allowed on Notre Dame property.
	\item Call 911 if anything seems suspicious - don't worry if you should be calling ND or SB police, just call 911 and get help.
\end{enumerate}
