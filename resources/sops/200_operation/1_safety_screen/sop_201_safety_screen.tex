\section{Summary}
All individuals must be screened before they enter the Magnet Room. This document establishes MRI Safety screening procedures at the Notre Dame Human Neuroimaging Center (ND-HNC).


\section{Scope}
These policies apply to all individuals who enter Zones 3 \& 4 (B15, B17, B17a; Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).


\section{Definitions}

\begin{itemize}
	\item \MagnetRoom
	\item \NDHNC
	\item \Operator
	\item \Visitor
\end{itemize}

\section{Notes}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Emergency response and maintenance personnel \textbf{CANNOT} enter the Magnet Room unless they have been screened and all metal objects have been removed.
	\item Emergency response personnel can \textbf{ONLY} enter Zone 3 without completing an MRI Safety screener if the door to the Magnet Room has been locked by key.
	\item ND-HNC only stores MRI Safety forms for staff and researchers. Visitor forms are maintained by their respective research teams.
\end{itemize}


\section{Policies \& Procedures}

The purpose of MRI Safety screening is to determine whether entry into the Magnet Room poses a health or safety risk. Accordingly, all individuals entering Zones 3 \& 4 must be screened according to the procedures below.

%
\subsection{MRI Prescreen}
\label{ssec:mri-pre}
Prescreening is conducted for Visitor's intending to enter the Magnet Room (e.g. a research participant). Before arriving at the ND-HNC, research personnel check for potential MRI contraindications with the prospective Vistor:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Non-MRI compatible medical implants.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Note}: Visitors with an implant must present documentation from their surgeon's office that the device is MRI compatible.
        \item \textbf{IUDs}: North American non-metallic intrauterine devices (e.g. Mirena) are approved. Metallic (e.g. Cu) and IUDs from other regions must be approved by the MRI Technologist.
    \end{itemize}
    \item Other metal embedded in the body (e.g. shrapnel, unremovable piercings).
    \item Pregnancy.
    \item Claustrophobia (the Mock can be used to assess comfort levels).
    \item Tattoo on face or neck, including permanent makeup.
\end{itemize}

%
\subsection{MRI Preparation}
\label{ssec:mri-prep}
Visitors intending to enter the Magnet Room (e.g. a research participant) should be informed of the following restrictions, before arriving at the ND-HNC, to guide their preparations:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Clothing should be free of metallic objects and clasps; bras should not have an underwire. Anti-odorant materials (e.g. Lululemon's Silverescent) are not MRI compatible.
    \item Jewelry will need to be removed, including piercings if they are metallic.
    \item Cosmetics such as colored contacts, magnetic lashes, and makeup or nail polish with glitter will have to be removed. Tanning lotions are not MRI compatible.
    \item Hair must be free of all accessories (metallic clips, barrettes, scrunchees, etc.).
    \item If Visitors have corrected vision, they should wear contacts or know their prescription.
\end{itemize}

%
\subsection{MRI Safety Screen}
\label{ssec:mri-safety}
All who enter Zone 3, regardless of whether they will enter Zone 4 (Magnet Room) fill out Form 001: MRI Safety. ND-HNC staff and research personnel submit a form (a) annually, and (b) whenever a change occurs in their MRI compatibility. Visitors fill out the form in Zone 1, and researchers with at least Level 2 access should be available to answer any questions.

\begin{itemize}
    \item For Visitors, Form 001 must be filled out on the same day as entering Zone 3.
    \item Each question/prompt must be answered individually.
    \item In the case of minors, their legal guardian must fill out the form.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Note: verify guardianship, which may differ from the individual who brought the minor to the center.
    \end{itemize}
    \item The Visitor signs and dates the form in the designated location.
\end{itemize}


\subsection{MRI Safety Screen, Review 1}
\label{ssec:mri-rev1}
A researcher with at least Level 2 access will review the Visitor's responses on Form 001.

\begin{itemize}
    \item Discuss any potential contraindications, points of confusion, or concerns. Inform the Operator about any items discussed or addressed.
    \item After reviewing Form 001, the researcher prints and signs their name in the designated location.
    \item If the researcher determines the Visitor is MRI compatible, they may escort the Visitor into Zones 2 \& 3.
\end{itemize}

% Screen Two
\subsection{MRI Safety Screen, Review 2}
\label{ssec:mri-rev2}
A second review is required for Visitors entering Zone 4 (Magnet Room).

\begin{itemize}
    \item The Operator conducts the second review, who must not be the same person that conducted Review 1.
    \item Any potential contraindications, points of confusion, or concerns are addressed, including those raise by the first reviewer
    \item The Operator signs and dates Form 001.
    \item The Visitor may enter Zone 4 only after the Operator has determined that it is safe to do so.
\end{itemize}
