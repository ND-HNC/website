\section{Summary}
This document establishes MRI Safety Zones of the research suite at the Notre Dame Human Neuroimaging Center (ND-HNC). These Zones follow the American College of Radiology guidelines and are designed to ensure that Zone access is commensurate with training and experience. Roles and restrictions for each level of access are also described.


\section{Scope}
These policies apply to all investigators, research assistants, and ND-HNC staff who enter Zones 3 \& 4 (B15, B17, B17a; Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).

% research suite
\subsection{Research Suite}
\label{ssec:res-suite}

The ND-HNC research suite consists of the following rooms and areas (see Fig. \ref{fig:ndhnc-layout}):

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Reception (B04)
	\item Staff offices (B28, B32, B34)
	\item Data processing and conference (B30, B26)
	\item Interview and changing (B05-06, B10-11)
	\item EEG: preparation, testing, and control (B19-22)
	\item TMS: administration (B23)
	\item MRI: simulator (B14)
	\item MRI: vestibule, equipment, control (B15, B17-18)
	\item MRI: magnet room (B17a)
\end{enumerate}

These are located on the lower level of the Veldman Family Psychology Clinic. See SOP 301 for access procedures.


% Zones
\subsection{Safety Zones}
\label{ssec:safety-zones}

The research suite is organized into four Safety Zones (Figs. \ref{fig:mri-zones}, \ref{fig:mri-zones-diag}). See section \ref{ssec:access-levels} for description of Access Levels.

\subsubsection{Zone 1}
\label{sssec:safety-zones-1}

This area constitutes the reception area, staff offices, conference room, and data processing room (\ref{ssec:res-suite}, 1-3). The reception area is accessible from the building's main reception and is used as a waiting area for researchers and participants. A bathroom and consultation rooms are accessible from the waiting area. The staff offices, conference room, and data processing room are accessible from the staff entrance and are used for ND-HNC related administration, research, and meetings. A break room and staff bathroom are also located in this zone.

MRI safety screening is \textbf{NOT} required for entry into Zone 1. This is typically the zone where such screening is conducted.

\subsubsection{Zone 2}
\label{sssec:safety-zones-2}

This area consists of changing rooms, a bathroom and shower, interview rooms, MRI simulator, EEG, and TMS rooms (\ref{ssec:res-suite}, 4-7). Zone 2 is accessed from Zone 1 with the Irish1Card. Zone 2 is used for research protocols that do not involve the MRI machine (e.g. behavioral testing, clinical assessment, EEG, and TMS). MRI safety screening can be conducted in Zone 2, but is typically done in Zone 1. Participants and visitors must be escorted in this Zone by a researcher with at least Level 1 access.

MRI safety screening is \textbf{NOT} required for entry into Zone 2 when the participant will not contribute MRI data.


\subsubsection{Zone 3}
\label{sssec:safety-zones-3}

This area consists of the MRI vestibule, equipment, and control rooms (\ref{ssec:res-suite}, 8). Zone 3 is accessed from Zone 2 with the Irish1Card. This area is used for collecting MRI data and maintaining MR-related hardware. Researchers with Level 2 access can enter this area for MRI-related purposes when supervised, and participants and visitors must be escorted by a researcher with at least Level 3 access.

MRI safety screening \textbf{IS} required for entry into Zone 3, and the screening form must have been reviewed and approved by a researcher with at least Level 2 access.


\subsubsection{Zone 4}
\label{sssec:safety-zones-4}

This area consists of the Magnet Room (\ref{ssec:res-suite}, 9), where the MRI machine is located. Zone 4 is accessed from the Control Room (Zone 3). Researchers with Level 3 access may enter this zone with permission, and must be supervised by the MRI Technologist or a researcher with Level 4 access.

MRI safety screening \textbf{IS} required for entrance into Zone 4, and this screening must have been reviewed and approved by the MRI Technologist or a researcher with Level 4 access (in addition to the Level 2-3 approval noted above).


% Levels
\subsection{Access Levels}
\label{ssec:access-levels}

Access to the ND-HNC research suite is restricted to research personnel, participants, and legal guardians. Anyone with building access is able to visit Zone 1. Access to additional Zones requires a demonstrated and current need as well as additional training and certifications.

\subsubsection{Level 1}
\label{sssec:access-levels-1}

Level 1 researchers may enter Zone 2 in order to access the interview, EEG, and TMS rooms. They escort visitors and participants in these areas. Their level of access relates to non-MRI activities.

Requirements:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Complete annual ND-HNC MRI Safety training.
	\item Participate in building access and safety trainings (SOP 300s).
	\item Wear Level 1 access badge while in the ND-HNC research suite.
\end{itemize}

Recommendations:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Complete first-aid and CPR training.
\end{itemize}

Privileges:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Unsupervised access to Zone 2.
	\item Escort participants and Visitors in Zone 2.
\end{itemize}

Restrictions:
\begin{itemize}
	\item No access to Mock Room (B14).
	\item No access to Zone 3 (B15, B17, B18).
	\item No access to Zone 4 (B17a).
	\item Cannot review MRI Safety forms.
\end{itemize}


\subsubsection{Level 2}
\label{sssec:access-levels-2}

Level 2 researchers may enter Zone 3, under the supervision of a researcher with at least Level 3 access, to assist in MRI-related research activities; they have access to the Stimulus Control and DICOM computers. They may also enter Zone 4 under the supervision of the MRI Technologist or a researcher with Level 4 access. Level 2 researchers have unsupervised access to the Mock room and can review and sign MRI Safety forms. Level 2 researchers are undergraduate and graduate students and other research staff, most research assistants in the MRI suite are Level 2.

Requirements:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Complete annual ND-HNC MRI Safety training.
	\item Complete annual first-aid and CPR training
	\item Annually provide an MRI Safety form.
	\item Participate in regular refresher trainings.
	\item Wear Level 2 access badge while in the ND-HNC research suite.
\end{itemize}

Privileges:
\begin{itemize}
	\item All Level 1 privileges.
	\item Access and use of Mock Scanner and Mock computer.
	\item Supervised access to Zones 3 \& 4.
	\item Access and use of Stimulus Control and DICOM computers.
	\item Review and sign MRI Safety forms.
	\item Help un/load participants into the MRI scanner.
	\item Can be the Helper in Emergency Responses (see SOP 100s).
\end{itemize}

Restrictions:
\begin{itemize}
	\item No access to Zone 3 Equipment Room.
	\item Irish1Card access to Zone 3 is not granted.
\end{itemize}


\subsubsection{Level 3}
\label{sssec:access-levels-3}

Level 3 researchers are more independent in Zone 3 and may access Zone 4 when supervised by the MRI Technologist or a researcher with Level 4 access. They are post-baccalaureate research staff, graduate students, post-doctoral fellows, and faculty. They are typically the Helper in Emergency SOPs but can be the Lead when they are the researcher with the highest level of access. Level 3 researchers may train to become an MRI operator (Level 4) for project-specific reasons (e.g. weekend pediatric scanning).

Requirements:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Complete annual ND-HNC MRI Safety training.
	\item Complete annual first-aid and CPR training
	\item Annually provide an MRI Safety form.
	\item Pass Emergency SOP exam.
	\item Participate in regular refresher trainings.
	\item Wear Level 3 access badge while in the ND-HNC research suite.
	\item Current Notre Dame graduate student, post-doctoral fellow, or faculty.
	\item Complete 30 hours of research assistance in Zone 3 as Level 2.
\end{itemize}

Privileges:
\begin{itemize}
	\item All Level 2 privileges.
	\item May supervise Level 2.
	\item Unsupervised access to Zones 3.
	\item Supervised access to Zone 4.
	\item Can be the Helper or Lead in Emergency Responses (see SOP 100s).
	\item When training for Level 4 and supervised:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Access MRI Control computer.
		\item Operate the MRI scanner.
		\item Perform start-up/shutdown procedures.
		\item Perform QA/QC.
		\item Access MRI Equipment room.
	\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

Restrictions:
\begin{itemize}
	\item No unsupervised access to Zone 4.
	\item Cannot be second reviewer of MRI Safety form.
\end{itemize}


\subsubsection{Level 4}
\label{sssec:access-levels-4}

Level 5 researchers are independent in the MRI research suite, but operate under guidance and with permission from the MRI Technologist. They are the Lead in Emergency SOPs, and can be the second reviewer of participant MRI Safety forms (but must not be the same person that performed the first review). They are Ph.D. candidates, post-doctoral fellows, and faculty.

Requirements:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Pass operator exam.
	\item Pass emergency SOP exam.
	\item Participate in maintenance training.
	\item Wear Level 4 access badge while in the ND-HNC research suite.
	\item Current Notre Dame Ph.D. candidate, post-doctoral fellow, or faculty.
	\item Complete 30 hours of training as Level 3.
\end{itemize}

Privileges:
\begin{itemize}
	\item All Level 3 privileges.
	\item Unsupervised access to Zones 3 \& 4.
	\item Lead in Emergency Responses (see SOP 100s).
	\item Independently perform:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Scan participants.
		\item Start-up and shutdown procedures.
		\item QA/QC scans.
		\item Troubleshoot issues.
		\item Maintain relevant logs.
	\end{itemize}
	\item Coordinate with ND-HNC staff
	\item Train Levels 2-3.
\end{itemize}

Restrictions:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Must be accompanied by a second person of at least Level 2 access when a participant or visitor is in Zone 4.
	\item Operates at the discretion of the MRI Technologist and other ND-HNC staff.
\end{itemize}


% Staff access
\subsection{Staff Access}
\label{ssec:staff-access}

Staff employed by the ND-HNC have unsupervised access to the MRI suite (Zones 1-4), and access the Zones in order to fulfill their respective job responsibilities. They promote an environment of safety and accountability, enforce Standard Operating Policies, and report compliance issues. Staff maintain and participate in MRI Safety and SOP trainings, first-aid and CPR certifications, and submit an MRI Safety form annually. In an emergency, they can assist as Lead or Helper. The Siemens Customer Service Engineer also has unsupervised access to Zones 1-4.
