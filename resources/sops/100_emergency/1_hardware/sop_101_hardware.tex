\section{Summary}
This document establishes procedures for hardware emergencies at the Notre Dame Human Neuroimaging Center (ND-HNC).

\section{Scope}
These policies apply to all investigators, research assistants, and ND-HNC staff who enter Zones 3 \& 4 (B15, B17, B17a; Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).

\section{Definitions}

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Subject}: An individual who is participating in an experimental protocol at ND-HNC and has not taken the ND-HNC safety course.
	\item \textbf{Experimenter}: The investigator, research assistant, or MRI operator who is responsible for the subject and conducting the experiment.
	\item \textbf{Magnet Room}: B17A; The room within the MRI suite that contains the MRI scanner. It is accessed from within the MRI Console room (B17) through a doorway that is controlled by an electronic lock.
	\item \textbf{Control Room}: B17; The main room of the MRI suite that contains the computer displays and keyboards that control the MRI scanner. The console room is accessed from B15 that is controlled by an electronic lock.
	\item \textbf{Electronic Lock}: All doors in the ND-HNC MRI center are controlled by electronic locks. The electronic lock system recognizes the owner's Irish1Card, logs the time of the attempted entry, and unlocks the door if the owner has been granted access.
\end{itemize}

\section{Notes}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Emergency response and maintenance personnel \textbf{CANNOT} enter the Magnet Room unless they have been screened and all metal objects have been removed.
	\item Fire extinguishers in the MRI suite are \textbf{NOT} MRI compatible, and cannot be brought into the Magnet Room.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Policies \& Procedures}

In the sections below, several scenarios are depicted regarding hardware emergencies.


% Gas
\subsection{Gas: the Magnet Room is filling with gas and/or vapor.}
\label{ssec:gas}

A magnet quench results in the rapid boil-off of cryogens that maintain the superconductivity of the main field coils. While the resulting gases are engineered to exhaust safely through ceiling-mounted vent pipes, a venting system failure will cause these gases to accumulate in the room. Although the cryogens are non-toxic and non-explosive, they present a critical safety risk by displacing oxygen, creating a severe danger of fatal asphyxiation.

\subsubsection*{\textit{Single Responder}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item If a Subject is in the scanner, remove them immediately. If the Subject is injured, follow the procedures in SOP 2.
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Stay low to the floor below the gas. The room will be cold, water vapor may make visibility in the room poor. A change in pressure may make it difficult to open the Magnet Room door.
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Lock the Magnet Room door, move to Zone 2 (Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).
	\item Call 911 to notify the South Bend Fire Department (SBFD) and say ``We have a possible gas leak in the MRI Center at B05 Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Once in progress, a quench cannot be stopped. Do not attempt any interventions and stay out of the Magnet Room.
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Call Veldman Reception and any MRI staff.
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item MRI staff: call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer).
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Prepare to receive and escort SBFD.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{\textit{Dual Responders}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Person One (Level 4)
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item If a Subject is in the scanner, remove them immediately. If the Subject is injured, follow the procedures in SOP 2.
		\item Lock the Magnet Room door, move to Zone 2 (Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).
		\item Prepare to receive and escort SBFD.
	\end{enumerate}

	\item Person Two (Level 2-3)
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Move to Zone 2 (Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).
		\item Call 911 to notify the South Bend Fire Department (SBFD) and say ``We have a possible gas leak in the MRI Center at B05 Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
		\item Call Veldman Reception and any MRI staff.
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item MRI staff: call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer).
		\end{enumerate}
		\item Assist in receiving, escorting SBFD.
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Fire
\subsection{Fire or Smoke: present in Magnet or Console Room.}
\label{ssec:fire}

Electrical disruption may spark combustible materials, resulting in fire or smoking. Plumbing failure or severe storms may result in flooding and electrical disruption. The Magnet Room is equipped with built-in fire suppression hardware.

\subsubsection*{\textit{Single Responder}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Immediately press the Emergency Power Off (EPO) button (Fig. \ref{fig:button-wall}).
	\item If a Subject is in the scanner, remove them immediately. If the Subject is injured, follow the procedures in SOP 2.
	\item If possible, lock the Magnet Room door.
	\item \textbf{Fire in Magnet Room}: Do not fight.
	\item \textbf{Fire in Console Room}: If possible, use the fire extinguisher in B15 to extinguish the fire (Fig. \ref{fig:fire-ext}).
	\item Pull fire alarm (Fig. \ref{fig:fire-alarm}).
	\item Leave the building.
	\item Call 911 to notify the South Bend Fire Department (SBFD) and say: ``We have [fire, smoke] in the MRI Center at B05 Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
	\item Call Veldman Reception and any MRI staff.
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item MRI staff: call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer).
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Prepare to receive and escort SBFD, Police.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{\textit{Dual Responders}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Person One (Level 4)
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Immediately press the Emergency Power Off (EPO) button (Fig. \ref{fig:button-wall}).
		\item If a Subject is in the scanner, remove them immediately. If the Subject is injured, follow the procedures in SOP 2.
		\item If possible, lock the Magnet Room door.
		\item Leave the building.
		\item Prepare to receive and escort SBFD, Water, Police.
	\end{enumerate}

	\item Person Two (Level 2-3)
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Pull fire alarm (Fig. \ref{fig:fire-alarm}).
		\item \textbf{Fire in Magnet Room}: Do not fight.
		\item \textbf{Fire in Console Room}: If possible, use the fire extinguisher in B15 to extinguish the fire (Fig. \ref{fig:fire-ext}).
		\item Pull fire alarm (Fig. \ref{fig:fire-alarm}).
		\item Leave the building.
		\item Call 911 to notify the South Bend Fire Department (SBFD) and say: ``We have [fire, smoke] in the MRI Center at B05 Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
		\item Call Veldman Reception and any MRI staff.
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item MRI staff: call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer).
		\end{enumerate}
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{O2 Sensor: Alarms.}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Follow the relevant Gas (SOP 101.5.1) or Fire, Smoke (SOP 101.5.2) protocol.
\end{enumerate}


% Metal no subject
\subsection{Metal: an object is attracted to the magnet, a Subject is NOT in the bore.}
\label{ssec:metal-no-subj}

Any ferrous material introduced into the Magnet Room will become attracted to the bore. Objects may range in size from the very small (metallic shavings, paper clips) to the intermediate (tools, watch, phone) and the large (chair, fire extinguisher). The very small items will disrupt signal, compromising data quality, while the larger items pose a risk to the magnet itself.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Do not attempt to remove the object.
	\item Call any MRI staff.
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item MRI staff: call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer).
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Metal unaffected subject
\subsection{Metal: an object is attracted to the magnet, an UNAFFECTED Subject is in the bore.}
\label{ssec:metal-un-subj}

When a research assistant or MRI personnel enter the Magnet Room during the course of research, perhaps to calibrate the eye tracker or reattach psychophysiologic recording devices, there is a risk that they will introduce a ferrous object into the field.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Remove the Subject from the Magnet Room.
	\item Follow procedures in SOP 101.5.5.
\end{enumerate}


% Pump
\subsection{Pump: you do not hear the pumping.}
\label{ssec:pump}

The pumps circulate the cryogens, which can be heard in B15, B17a, and B18. If the pumps are inoperative for a long enough period, the magnet may lose superconductivity and quench.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Call Christine Schmidt (Haskris).
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Haskris monitors the cooling system and should contact MRI staff for any issues
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Call any MRI staff.
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item MRI staff: call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer).
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Lock out
\subsection{Access: you are locked out of the Console or Magnet Room.}
\label{ssec:access}

The Console Room door is controlled by an Electronic Lock and will not open unless an authorized Irish1Card is presented. The Magnet Room door is electronically sealed. In the case of power failure, a key can open each door.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Door to Magnet Room (B17a) does not release: press the emergency release button (Fig. \ref{fig:button-wall}).
	\item Door to Console Room (B17 |> B15) does not open: press the emergency release button (Fig. \ref{fig:button-wall}).
	\item Door to Console Room (B17 <| B15) does not open: ensure you have proper access by contacting Allison Collins (Building Management).
\end{enumerate}
