\section{Summary}
This document establishes procedures for medical emergencies at the Notre Dame Human Neuroimaging Center (ND-HNC) MRI scanner. They are intended to coordinate a planned response of:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Moving to safety,
	\item Initiating emergency response,
	\item Providing First Aid,
	\item Coordinating with others in the building,
	\item Protecting responders from the magnetic field.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scope}
These policies apply to all investigators, research assistants, and ND-HNC staff who enter Zones 3 \& 4 (B15, B17, B17a; Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).

\section{Definitions}

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Subject}: An individual who is participating in an experimental protocol at ND-HNC and has not taken the ND-HNC safety course.
	\item \textbf{Lead}: The individual responsible for the Subject's safety and running the MRI protocols.
	\item \textbf{Helper}: Second person, when available, who assists in experimental operations.
	\item \textbf{Magnet Room}: B17A; The room within the MRI suite that contains the MRI scanner. It is accessed from within the MRI Console room (B17) through a doorway that is controlled by an electronic lock.
	\item \textbf{Control Room}: B17; The main room of the MRI suite that contains the computer displays and keyboards that control the MRI scanner. The console room is accessed from B15 that is controlled by an electronic lock.
	\item \textbf{Electronic Lock}: All doors in the ND-HNC MRI center are controlled by electronic locks. The electronic lock system recognizes the owner's Irish1Card, logs the time of the attempted entry, and unlocks the door if the owner has been granted access.
\end{itemize}

\section{Notes}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Emergency response and maintenance personnel \textbf{CANNOT} enter the Magnet Room unless they have been screened and all metal objects have been removed.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Policies \& Procedures}

In the sections below, several scenarios are depicted regarding medical emergencies.


% Injury
\subsection{Injury: an individual sustains an injury requiring medical attention.}
\label{ssec:injury}

The number of staff, researchers, and participants involved in research at the ND-HNC results in a non-zero probability that an accident or event requiring medical attention will occur.

\subsubsection*{\textit{Single Responder}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Conscious individual}: escort them to B15 (Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).
	\item \textbf{Unconscious individual in Console Room}: move them into the recovery position.
	\item \textbf{Unconscious individual in Magnet Room}: use MR compatible gurney to move them to B15 [TODO PIC].
	\item Call 911 and say: ``We have a person that needs medical attention in the MRI Center at B05 Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
	\item Call Veldman Reception and say ``We have a medical emergency and have called 911.''
	\item Retrieve first-aid kit, administer first aid.
	\item Lock Magnet Room door.
	\item Prepare to receive and escort emergency medical services.
	\item Call any MRI staff.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{\textit{Dual Responders}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Lead (Level 4)
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item \textbf{Conscious individual}: escort them to B15 (Fig. \ref{fig:mri-zones}).
		\item \textbf{Unconscious individual in Console Room}: move them into the recovery position.
		\item \textbf{Unconscious individual in Magnet Room}: use MR compatible gurney to move them to B15 [TODO PIC].
		\item Retrieve first-aid kit, administer first aid.
		\item Lock Magnet Room door.
		\item Prepare to receive and escort emergency medical services.
		\item Call any MRI staff.
	\end{enumerate}

	\item Helper (Level 1-3)
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Call 911 and say: ``We have a person that needs medical attention in the MRI Center at B05 Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
		\item Call Veldman Reception and say ``We have a medical emergency and have called 911.''
		\item Assist in first aid.
		\item Assist receiving and escorting emergency medical services.
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
