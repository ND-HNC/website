\section{Summary}
This document establishes procedures for medical emergencies at the Notre Dame Human Neuroimaging Center (ND-HNC) MRI scanner. They are intended to coordinate a planned response of:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Moving to safety,
	\item Initiating emergency response,
	\item Providing First Aid,
	\item Coordinating with others in the building,
	\item Protecting responders from the magnetic field.
\end{enumerate}

For dual responders, the Lead protects the participant and then equipment while the Helper initiates the emergency response.

\section{Scope}
These policies apply to all investigators, research assistants, and ND-HNC staff who enter Zones 3 \& 4 (B15, B17, B17a; Form RO03).

\section{Notes}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Emergency response and maintenance personnel \textbf{CANNOT} enter the Magnet Room unless they have been screened and all metal objects have been removed.
	\item Emergency response personnel can \textbf{ONLY} enter Zone 3 without completing an MRI Safety screener if the door to the Magnet Room has been locked by key.
	\item The First-Aid kit and Automated External Defibrillator (AED) are \textbf{NOT} MRI compatible.
\end{enumerate}

% Injury-conscious
\subsection{Injury: a \textbf{CONSCIOUS} individual sustains an injury requiring medical attention.}
\label{ssec:injury-cons}

Given the volume of staff, researchers, and participants at the ND-HNC, there is a realistic possibility that an accident or medical emergency will occur. Accordingly, the center must maintain a constant state of readiness to respond. Ensuring safety means having clear medical protocols in place so that any incident is handled swiftly and effectively.

\subsubsection*{\textit{Single Responder}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Escort them to B15 (Form RO03).
	\item Call 911 and say: ``We have a person that needs medical attention in the MRI Center at Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
	\item Call Veldman Reception and say ``We have a medical emergency and have called 911.''
	\item Retrieve first-aid kit, administer first aid [TODO PIC].
	\item Close and lock Magnet Room door.
	\item Prepare to receive and escort emergency medical services.
	\item Call any MRI staff.
	\item Record Adverse Event in ND-HNC Form LG03: Adverse Event.
\end{enumerate}

It is absolutely critical that we take any symptoms seriously and act immediately. % Metal, no risk
\subsection{Metal: pinned Visitor \textbf{WITHOUT} risk to life or limb}
\label{ssec:metal-no-risk}

Inexperience, complacency, and/or over-confidence when entering the Magnet Room can inadvertently introduce ferromagnetic materials into the magnetic field. When a Visitor is present, they may be pinned to the bore by the materials. This SOP details what to do if there is not any risk to the Visitor's life or limb i.e. an injury has not occurred or is very minor.

\subsubsection*{\textit{Single Responder}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Do not attempt to remove the item.
	\item Visitor is injured: provide first aid.
	\item Call any MRI staff; MRI staff call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer) to determine if the magnet must be quenched.
	\item Record Adverse Event in ND-HNC Form LG03: Adverse Event.
	\item Record compliance issue in ND-HNC Form LG04: Compliance.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{\textit{Dual Responders}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Lead
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Do not attempt to remove the item.
		\item Call any MRI staff; MRI staff call Richard Vega (Siemens Engineer) to determine if the magnet must be quenched.
		\item Record Adverse Event in ND-HNC Form LG03: Adverse Event.
		\item Record compliance issue in ND-HNC Form LG04: Compliance.
	\end{enumerate}

	\item Helper
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Visitor is injured: provide first aid.
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


% Metal, risk
\subsection{Metal: pinned Visitor \textbf{WITH} risk to life or limb}
\label{ssec:metal-risk}

Inexperience, complacency, and/or over-confidence when entering the Magnet Room can inadvertently introduce ferromagnetic materials into the magnetic field. When a Visitor is present, they may be pinned to the bore by the materials. This SOP details what to do when risks to the Visitor's life or limb exists i.e. injury has occurred, or is likely to occur.

\subsubsection*{\textit{Single Responder}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Quench the magnet (Fig. \ref{fig:quench-con}; Fig. \ref{fig:quench-mag}, A).
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item A quench cannot be stopped once it is initiated.
		\item As the helium boils off, the magnet will decrease in strength.
		\item It will take approximately 3 minutes for the magnet to deactivate.
	\end{enumerate}
	\item Call 911 and say: ``We have a person that needs medical attention in the MRI Center at Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
	\item Call Veldman Reception and say ``We have a medical emergency and have called 911.''
	\item Retrieve First-Aid kit.
	\item If gas fails to vent, once magnet is ramped down use MRI gurney and follow SOP 101.\ref{101-ssec:gas}.
	\item Administer first aid.
	\item Prepare to receive and escort emergency medical services.
	\item Call any MRI staff.
	\item Record Adverse Event in ND-HNC Form LG03: Adverse Event.
	\item Record compliance issue in ND-HNC Form LG04: Compliance.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{\textit{Dual Responders}}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Lead
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Quench the magnet (Fig. \ref{fig:quench-con}; Fig. \ref{fig:quench-mag}, A).
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item A quench cannot be stopped once it is initiated.
			\item As the helium boils off, the magnet will decrease in strength.
			\item It will take approximately 3 minutes for the magnet to deactivate.
		\end{enumerate}
		\item If gas fails to vent, once magnet is ramped down use MRI gurney and follow SOP 101.\ref{101-ssec:gas}.
		\item Administer first aid.
		\item Call any MRI staff.
		\item Record Adverse Event in ND-HNC Form LG03: Adverse Event.
		\item Record compliance issue in ND-HNC Form LG04: Compliance.
	\end{enumerate}

	\item Helper
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Call 911 and say: ``We have a person that needs medical attention in the MRI Center at Veldman Family Psychology Clinic, 501 North Hill Street''.
		\item Call Veldman Reception and say ``We have a medical emergency and have called 911.''

% Seizure, Stroke
\subsection{Seizure or stroke}
\label{ssec:metal-seizure}

Visitors may have a complex medical history, and we need to be vigilant for, and responsive to, any signs of medical distress.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Seizure
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Do not attempt to hold person still.
		\item Provide cushioning for head.
		\item Follow relevant injury procedure (SOP 102.5.\ref{ssec:injury-uncons})
	\end{enumerate}

	\item Stroke
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Act F.A.S.T.
		\begin{itemize}
			\item F: Face - signs of drooping
			\item A: Arms - arm weakness
			\item S: Speech - difficulty speaking, slurring
			\item T: Time - initiate 911 immediately
		\end{itemize}
		\item Follow relevant injury procedure (SOP 102.5.\ref{ssec:injury-uncons})
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
